All-Weather Graphene Solar Panel Solutions

Consciously harnessing the power of the sun is the next step in humanity’s evolution. Solar power is not only the future of energy, it is also the future of our economy and of consciousness as our collective intention penetrates the next frontier of planetary awareness. Utilizing the liberation rays of the sun to serve humanity’s energy needs creates a shift that not only reduces our carbon footprint, improves our air & water quality, but also signals an evolution in how we perceive our relationship with the natural cosmic forces within our solar system and beyond.

Graphene Solar Panels are the future of energy. Graphene solar panels can deliver up to 40% more electricity as traditional solar panels. PV Graph utilizes a hybrid cell technology that combines thin film tech with crystalline silicon tech to produce up to 30% higher efficiency modules that last longer, are less prone to micro-damage, and even when damaged, still work with almost zero loss of productivity.

Our Global partners at Poland-based FreeVolt™ have made an industry game-changing breakthrough. They are the first company in the world to have patented solar technology using Graphene. The discovery of Graphene in 2010 was awarded a Nobel Prize. The material is up to 200 times stronger than steel, is 97% transparent and conducts electricity better than copper.

Graphene’s unique electrical properties enable these all-weather solar panels the ability to generate electricity. The positively charged ions of rain water, including calcium sodium, and ammonium, contact the graphene surface of the solar cell while the negatively charged ions from the salt permeate the layer of graphene, enriching it with electrons. The difference between the positively charged water on the surface of the cell and the negative charge of the graphene layer then produces the potential needed for electrical current to flow. This double layer of electrical charges produced by the rainwater is called a pseudocapacitor. Capacitors are electrical components that store electrical energy. In this case, electrical energy is derived by splitting the positively charged ions in rainwater from the negatively charged ions.

Graphene solar panels are thus able to meet the rising demand for all-weather solar panels capable of addressing evolving coastal climates, that are more energy efficient and more durable than traditional solar panels.

According to the UN Global E-waste Monitor 2020, e-waste is now the world’s fastest growing domestic waste stream. E-waste is referred to as the world’s fastest growing solid waste stream. Since 2000, e-waste amounts have grown from 20 million to 50 million tons per year. In 2019, a record 53.6 million metric tons of electronic waste was generated worldwide. If society’s unsustainable approach to electronic equipment and device management remains unaddressed, this rising tide of e-waste is forecast more than double by 2050, reaching approximately 111 million tons.
